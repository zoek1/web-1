Read the rules of a Gitcoin hackathon here

Whether you are a novice or experienced designer, developer, or curious learner, all are welcome to participate in a Gitcoin Hackathon.

Hackers are welcome to work alone or with a team - unless otherwise specified,. All work must be original and developed during the hackathon prize period.

Each hackathon “Bounty” represents a unique challenge and/or prize - hackers can work on multiple bounties, but only one submissions is allowed per bounty unless otherwise specified.

Projects must be submitted officially through the Gitcoin bounty page, before the hackathon end date and time. Make sure to check when the end is in your time zone. Sponsors do reserve the right to extend the deadline, however this must be communicated publicly to everyone to ensure fairness.

Typically hackathon projects must be entirely built during the length of the event, unless the bounty indicates you can use a pre-existing project with new additions.

Every bounty is unique with different criteria and requirements - make sure to read the details on the bounty page, and reach out to the sponsor or a Gitcoin staff member for help or Questions. Discord link: https://discord.gg/6PZUM3cFpz
